What did the researcher do?

The researcher located and analyzed academic research, media, governmental publications, and recently declassified national security documents on the environmental damage resulting from the production of nuclear weapons in the United States since the Manhattan Project. I identified historical and social factors that led to the American effort in the Cold War nuclear arms race. I document American efforts to "modernize" the nuclear arsenal and the ecological consequences for humanity and all biological life.

What did the researcher find?

The American military established a "treadmill of destruction mechanism" to compete in a nuclear arms race. In producing a nuclear arsenal, the military systematically privileged national security demands over the protection of human health and the environment.


How can you use this research?

In 2020, several countries are currently pursuing a new nuclear arms race. Governments and policy advocates can be reminded that historically, a nuclear arms race results in ecological atrocities at local, regional, and global scales. Understanding the conditions that allow a treadmill mechanism to emerge reveals the conditions that prevent it from becoming established. This research can therefore help scholars and policy makers craft national security strategies that take seriously the ecological threats posed by the creation of our own nuclear weapons. Environmental and nuclear disarmament organizations may use this research to inform their strategies for addressing the intersecting and impending catastrophes of global environmental change and nuclear weaponry.
